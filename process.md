# Production Methodology & Microclimate Focal Points

This document outlines the environmental and manual parameters utilized during the 2018 sun-drying phase to achieve the total metabolic stasis verified in COA #USG-2018Q5L2.

## 1. Geographic & Elevation Focal Point
* **Location:** Walnut Creek, California (Hills Region)
* **Target Elevation:** 105 feet above sea level
* **Atmospheric Advantage:** Sits precisely above the marine layer boundary, avoiding valley floor humidity traps and heavy evening dew points.

## 2. Solar & Atmospheric Timing (Solar Terms)
* **Xiazhi (Summer Solstice):** Peak solar radiation used to snap internal water tension immediately, triggering tissue vitrification without thermal degradation.
* **Dashu (Great Heat):** Utilized for its ultra-low baseline relative humidity, acting as a continuous atmospheric sponge.

## 3. Microclimate Moisture Variance Data

| Drying Phase / Time | Basin Floor Moisture (0–50 ft) | 105-Foot Hillside Moisture | Curcumin Stability Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Xiazhi (Solstice Noon)** | 45% (High humidity trap) | **12% (Flash Vitrification)** | Moisture snaps instantly; cellular tissue turns to "glass." |
| **Dashu (Peak Summer Heat)** | 35% (Stagnant heat) | **8% (Maximum Dryness)** | The surrounding atmosphere pulls out remaining moisture. |
| **Midnight Recovery** | 75% (Heavy dew point) | **14% (Dew Shield Zone)** | Hillside prevents moisture re-absorption. |

## 4. Critical Manual Operations
* **Manual Raking & Turning:** Curcumin cubes are hand-turned on the curing trays at scheduled intervals.
* **Purpose:** Distributes the thermal load to prevent UV scorching of active curcuminoids and breaks bottom-side vapor traps for uniform drying.
