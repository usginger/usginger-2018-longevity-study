# 🏺 9-Year Metabolic Stasis Storage Protocol

This document outlines the environmental controls and preservation methods used to maintain the active curcuminoid stability of the 2018 *Jiang-Huang* harvest. By halting organic degradation, this protocol ensures long-term molecular data integrity.

## 🧱 1. Biomass Preparation (Sun-Dried Cubes)
* **Harvest Processing**: Freshly harvested rhizomes were carefully cleaned and cut into uniform agricultural cubes.
* **Sun-Drying Phase**: Cubes were completely sun-dried to reduce moisture content below critical active degradation thresholds.
* **Moisture Matrix**: Final relative humidity of the processed biomass was locked at target storage baselines.

## 🌡️ 2. Macro-Environmental Settings
To keep the active molecules intact since 2018, the physical storage cells follow these strict boundaries:
* **Temperature Matrix**: Maintained a constant, cool temperature to prevent heat-induced compound breakdown.
* **Zero Light Exposure**: Absolute dark storage inside opaque containers to stop UV-light photolysis.
* **Atmospheric Sealing**: Hermetically sealed environments to eliminate ambient oxygen exchange and moisture spikes.

## 🧪 3. Longitudinal Stability Outcomes
* **No Preservatives**: The preservation relies purely on physical climate barriers rather than chemical additives.
* **Zero Contamination**: The storage method perfectly matches the zero-contamination footprint validated in **COA #USG-2018Q5L2**.
* **Molecular Retention**: LC-MS-MS testing streams confirm the curcuminoid molecules remain structurally locked over long timelines.
